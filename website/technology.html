Capabilities, investment and the evolution of agentic AI

AI Capabilities Today

Large language models (LLMs) have already reached professional benchmarks. GPT‑4 scored around 75 % on the U.S. bar exam and placed in the 90th percentile【877154183811370†L26-L30】, while ChatGPT performed at or near the passing threshold on all three stages of the USMLE medical boards【877154183811370†L26-L33】. Beyond chatbots, foundation models trained on millions of images can diagnose eye disease and even predict systemic conditions like heart failure【877154183811370†L32-L36】. LLMs can draft software, contracts and presentations from natural‑language instructions, effectively acting as coding copilots【877154183811370†L37-L39】.

However, AI also introduces new risks. Voice‑cloning systems can replicate a person’s speech from just a few seconds of audio, enabling plausible scams【877154183811370†L43-L47】. Malicious actors can spin up realistic chatbots to harvest payment details, and generative AI can doctor politicians’ speeches or create deepfakes to undermine elections【877154183811370†L142-L155】.

Investment & Economic Impact

Private AI investment in the United States reached $109.1 billion in 2024, nearly twelve times China’s investment and twenty‑four times the U.K.’s【240192331752712†L483-L489】. Hyperscale cloud providers doubled their projected AI capital expenditures between March 2024 and May 2025 (from $207 billion to $405 billion for 2026)【240192331752712†L497-L503】. OpenAI’s weekly average users skyrocketed from 180 million to over 800 million【240192331752712†L497-L503】.

Projections suggest AI could add $15.7 trillion to global GDP by 2030【240192331752712†L508-L524】. In advanced economies, AI may automate around 25 % of labour tasks【240192331752712†L530-L569】 and displace up to 83 million jobs by 2027 while creating 69 million new roles【240192331752712†L562-L569】. Productivity gains, combined with increased consumption, drive these forecasts【240192331752712†L520-L524】.

At the same time, high AI capital ratios can reduce labour demand, increasing unemployment and underemployment【877154183811370†L142-L139】. This section invites you to explore the tension between productivity gains and societal impacts.

Agentic AI & Swarm Architecture

LLMs do not “reason” like humans; they infer the most likely next word based on statistical patterns【877154183811370†L80-L86】. Nevertheless, emergent behaviours arise as models scale, and different agents built on the same base model can behave very differently depending on their training and memory retrieval strategies【877154183811370†L92-L106】.

A single‑agent architecture integrates memory, retrieval and tools into one workflow【877154183811370†L49-L60】, whereas a multi‑agent architecture employs an orchestrator to coordinate specialised agents performing concurrent tasks【877154183811370†L49-L60】. Key features of multi‑agent systems include autonomy, collaboration, competition and adaptability【877154183811370†L61-L64】.
